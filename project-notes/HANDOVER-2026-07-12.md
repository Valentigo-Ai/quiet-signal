## Who it's for

People managing chronic pain, PTSD, or other invisible conditions who struggle to
communicate day-to-day changes to family, carers, or clinical teams — especially when
talking about it out loud feels too hard. A private, near-instant way to say how they're
doing without re-explaining it each time.

## What it does

- A 15-second daily check-in (pain, anxiety/PTSD state, energy) with an optional note.
- Auto-generates a plain-language status message the person can edit before sharing.
- Shares privately with one or more trusted contacts via a dashboard (no one watches by default).
- Personal history/trends the patient can review.
- Optional journal with a nightly safety check for crisis language.

## Safety design (not diagnosis)

Explicitly **not** a diagnostic, treatment, or crisis-response tool — always signposts to
real crisis services. A crisis-language check runs before anything touches a journal entry;
flagged entries are excluded from further analysis. Crisis/support resources shown
automatically by country (UK, US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland) with correct
emergency and veteran-specific numbers. Full data export and account deletion any time.

## Privacy & cost

**No AI vendor anywhere in the stack** — message generation and journal insights are
rule-based/lexicon-based only, so health data is never sent to a third-party AI provider.
Running costs stay near £0/month. Row-level security enforced throughout.

## Architecture — three separate places (important mental model)

1. **App code** (React Native) → reaches devices only via: edit → local build (`.aab`) →
   manual upload to Play Console → release. Nothing automatic.
2. **Backend** (Supabase edge functions + database) → live instantly when deployed;
   independent of Play Console and app builds.
3. **Play Console** (store listing, testers, subscriptions, pricing) → edited directly in
   the browser; unrelated to the code.

Supabase edge functions currently deployed: `generate-message`, `share-checkin`,
`get-shared-message`, `nightly-journal-scan`, `delete-account`, `checkin-archive-scan`.

## Constraints / gotchas to remember
- **No EAS cloud builds** — out of monthly credits and can't afford more. All builds must be
  local (`eas build --platform android --profile production --local`).
- Local builds run via **WSL2**. A prior session added `%USERPROFILE%\.wslconfig`
  (`memory=10GB, processors=4, swap=4GB`) to stop the Linux OOM killer aborting builds
  (`[ABORT] Received termination signal.`); `wsl --shutdown` after editing it.
- Play Store requires **`.aab`** (app bundle) for new apps, not `.apk`. Use the `production`
  profile (has `app-bundle` in `eas.json`); `preview` produces `.apk` and gets rejected.
- Play Console developer account is under login index **u/2** (see identifiers above).
- Don't run `npm audit fix --force` — the 13 moderate advisories are transitive Expo/RN
  warnings; the force fix pulls breaking major bumps and breaks Expo builds.
- There's a pre-existing, unrelated line-ending-only change on `project-notes/powershell..txt`
  in the working tree — leave it out of commits.
